Founder Bio

Short Bio (100 words)

Christopher J. Bradley is a Gulf War veteran, lawyer, and civic educator based in Northwest Florida. After years of personal upheaval — including a painful estrangement from his daughter — he spent nearly a decade living on a sailboat, where he photographed over 2,000 sunsets and developed his guiding philosophy: "Love everyone, start with you." He's the author of a 28-book children's series on constitutional values and founder of several civic education platforms. The CJB Summit is his attempt to explore what connects people — starting with the 2,287 Americans who share his exact name.

Long Bio (350 words)

Christopher J. Bradley knows something about disconnection.

A Gulf War veteran who served with the 1st Cavalry Division driving a tank in combat at 19, Bradley returned home to build a life — law school, a legal career, a family. Then it fell apart. A difficult divorce led to estrangement from his daughter, a separation that persists to this day. The systems meant to protect families failed him, a story he documented in his memoir "The Citizen's Compass: A Journey Through American Principles and Personal Reckoning."

In the aftermath, Bradley did something unexpected: he moved onto a 36-foot sailboat docked along Florida's Northwest Gulf Coast. For nearly a decade, he lived on the water, often anchored less than a mile from where his daughter attended high school — close enough to hope, too far to reach.

During those years, he photographed over 2,000 sunsets from the same vantage point. The practice became a meditation on impermanence, beauty, and the stubborn persistence of showing up. He emerged with a simple philosophy: "Life is simple. Love everyone, start with you."

That philosophy now drives his work. Bradley is the author of "Liberty's Principles Pals," a 28-book children's series teaching constitutional values to young readers. He's built nine interconnected platforms focused on civic education, family strengthening, and intentional living — including The Citizen's Compass, The Art of Citizenship, and Love Everyone.love.

The Christopher J. Bradley Summit represents something new: an experiment in connection.

There are approximately 2,287 people in America who share his exact name — mathematicians, doctors, veterans, actors, teachers. They've never met. On July 4, 2026 — America's 250th birthday — Bradley is trying to bring as many of them together as possible.

"I've spent years thinking about what connects us and what divides us. This is the most literal version of that question I could imagine. Two thousand strangers who share nothing but a name, coming together to see what else we have in common."

The summit will be held in Northwest Florida and will benefit a veteran housing charity, honoring another Christopher Bradley — a Navy veteran from Crestview who was injured in service.
